Rat Special Abilities

  • Rats can wriggle through openings as small as 1/2" (1.3 cm) square.
  • Rats can saunter along horizontal wires easily while Roof Rats can even climb vertical wires
  • Rats can crawl up the inside of vertical pipes as small as 1.5" or 3.8 cm, in diameter.
  • Rats can shimmy up the outside of vertical pipes up to 3" or 7.6 cm, in diameter.
  • If plumbing pipes or electrical conduit are within 3" or 7.6 cm of a wall, rats can crawl up them, probably even wearing a tiny tool belt.
  • Rats can jump 36" or 0.9 m from a flat surface.
  • From a flat surface, rats can stretch to reach about 13" or 33 cm.
  • Even though they may be the last ones off a sinking ship, rats are able to dive and swim underwater for as long as 30 seconds. In open water, they can swim as far as 1/2 mile or 0.8 km. They can use these remarkable aquatic abilities to swim upward through waste pipes and into toilets.
  • Rats at the Dentist: Equipped with front teeth that continue to grow throughout their lives, rats are gnawing machines, attacking a vast array of materials from wood, lead pipes and cinder block to aluminum, glass, and adobe bricks.
